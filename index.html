Carbon Tracker

Did you know? Burning fossil fuels changes the climate more than any other human activity. Because we put more carbon dioxide into the atmosphere than natural processes can remove, the amount of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere increases every year. We can avoid much of the damage through a combination of decarbonizing our energy supply, pulling CO2 out the atmosphere, and developing more sustainable ways of thriving.


For each ton of carbon released into the atmosphere, 31 to 46 new trees are needed to return to carbon neutrality.
